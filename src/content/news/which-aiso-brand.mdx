Hub, Dev, Buzz, or Learn. The wrong pick wastes a quarter. Here is a short self-diagnostic to route you in under two minutes.

## How the test works

Three questions. The verb in your answer tells you the specialist.

The verb test: Hub **cites**. Dev **builds**. Buzz **says**. Learn **teaches**.

## Question 1 - Where do your customers look before they buy?

- **They ask ChatGPT, Perplexity, or Google AI Overviews.** → You need to be **cited**. Go to **AISO Hub**.
- **They use a tool, form, or system inside your business that is slow or broken.** → You need something **built**. Go to **AISO Dev**.
- **They scroll social. They follow accounts. They want to see your brand.** → You need content that **says** something. Go to **AISO Buzz**.
- **Not a customer problem - your own team needs to use AI safely and well.** → You need training that **teaches**. Go to **AISO Learn**.

If the first question already sorted you, stop reading. Go to the specialist. You are done.

## Question 2 - What breaks first if you do nothing this quarter?

- **AI search traffic stalls. Competitors get cited. You do not.** → Hub.
- **A manual process collapses under growth. The team burns out on copy-paste work.** → Dev.
- **Your social goes silent. Inbound dies because nobody knows you exist.** → Buzz.
- **The team uses AI ad-hoc, unsafely, or not at all - and the EU AI Act deadline is looming.** → Learn.

## Question 3 - Who owns the outcome internally?

- **Marketing / SEO / Content lead.** → Hub is your partner.
- **Operations / Product / Engineering lead.** → Dev is your partner.
- **Nobody. You need the marketing team.** → Buzz is your partner.
- **HR / People / Compliance lead.** → Learn is your partner.

If the answer is "nobody, and it is not marketing" - for example, nobody is leading operations - we are the wrong call for that problem. Honest answer. Go hire an ops lead first, come back after.

## Still torn between two

A few common cross-overs:

- **"I need content that is cited by AI and sounds like me."** Buzz writes the post. Hub makes sure AI cites it. They are designed to work together.
- **"I need a lead form built and then marketed."** Dev builds the form. Buzz drives traffic to it. Clean handoff.
- **"I need AI search work and internal tooling."** Hub first, Dev next quarter. Rarely both at once.
